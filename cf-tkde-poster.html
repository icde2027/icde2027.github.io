42nd IEEE International Conference on Data Engineering (ICDE) Montréal, Canada - May 4-8, 2026

Call for TKDE Poster

We are thrilled to invite you to the TKDE Poster Track at ICDE 2026, a collaborative effort between the IEEE International Conference on Data Engineering (ICDE) and the IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering (TKDE).

If your regular research article has been accepted for TKDE between December 1, 2024, and November 30, 2025, you are eligible to present your work in the form of a 2-page extended abstract in the proceedings of ICDE 2026 and a poster at the conference’s poster session. ICDE 2026 provides an excellent opportunity to connect with peers, industry leaders, and experts in the field. Make the most out of this opportunity and expand your professional network.

Selection Criteria

The article must align with the ICDE’s scope defined by the ICDE research track and industry track call-for-papers. The article must NOT be an extension of a paper published in another conference, symposium, or workshop.

Important Dates

All deadlines are 11:59PM PST.

  • Submission: December 09, 2025
  • Notification (Accept/Reject): February 9, 2026
  • Camera ready: March 1, 2026

Submission Guidelines

The posters should adhere to the ICDE 2026 proceeding formats for submissions. The poster title should be the title of your TKDE paper, followed by “Extended abstract”. The authors’ list should match that of the accepted TKDE paper. The extended abstract should be a summary of the TKDE paper without requiring any additional contributions. Include the TKDE paper’s acceptance details and attach an e-copy of your TKDE paper to the abstract submission. The submission should not exceed 2 pages and should be made on the Microsoft CMT: https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/TKDEPoster2026

Acknowledgment:
Microsoft CMT service was used for managing the peer-reviewing process for this conference. This service was provided for free by Microsoft and they bore all expenses, including costs for Azure cloud services as well as for software development and support.

Poster Size

Posters should be in the A0 size (84.1 cm × 118.9 cm), in a portrait layout. They can be affixed using double-sided tape.

Registration Policy

The extended abstract will appear in the ICDE 2026 proceedings under a special track for TKDE papers, and the authors must present the work at the ICDE poster session. Regarding the policy of ICDE registrations, the authors of an extended abstract (TKDE posters) will be treated in the same way as the authors of an ICDE paper. At least one of the authors must register for the conference before the camera-ready deadline, and this registration can be cancelled only after registration of the another author.

We are excited to see your contributions and looking forward to a vibrant exchange of ideas. Feel free to reach out with any questions.
